Mediterranean Meal Plan

Monday to Friday

Monday

Breakfast: Greek Yogurt Parfait
Ingredients (for 1):
  • 1 cup plain Greek yogurt
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 1/4 cup walnuts, roughly chopped
  • 1/2 cup mixed berries
Instructions:
  1. Spoon Greek yogurt into a bowl or glass.
  2. Drizzle honey over the yogurt.
  3. Sprinkle walnuts on top.
  4. Gently fold in or layer the mixed berries. Enjoy immediately.
Dinner: Lemon Herb Chicken with Quinoa and Sautéed Veggies (Yields leftovers for Tuesday’s lunch)
Ingredients (for 2):
  • 2 boneless, skinless chicken breasts (6 oz each)
  • 1 lemon (zest and juice)
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 tbsp olive oil (divided)
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/4 tsp black pepper
  • 1 cup quinoa (rinsed)
  • 2 cups water or low‐sodium chicken broth
  • 1 small head broccoli, cut into florets
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • Optional: Fresh parsley (for garnish)
Instructions:
  1. Marinate Chicken: In a shallow dish, combine 1 tbsp olive oil, lemon juice, lemon zest, minced garlic, oregano, salt, and pepper. Add chicken breasts; marinate 20 minutes (up to 1 hour in fridge).
  2. Prepare Quinoa: In a saucepan, combine quinoa and water (or broth). Bring to a boil, then reduce heat, cover, and simmer for 15 minutes. Fluff with a fork.
  3. Cook Chicken: Preheat a grill pan or skillet over medium-high heat. Cook chicken for 5–6 minutes per side until internal temperature reaches 165°F.
  4. Sauté Veggies: In a large skillet, heat 1 tbsp olive oil over medium heat. Add broccoli and sauté for 4–5 minutes. Add cherry tomatoes in the last 2 minutes. Season lightly with salt and pepper.
  5. Plate & Serve: Divide quinoa between two plates, slice chicken, and top with sautéed veggies. Garnish with fresh parsley if desired. Cool leftovers for Tuesday’s lunch.

Tuesday

Breakfast: Mediterranean Avocado Toast with Poached Egg
Ingredients (for 1):
  • 1 slice whole-grain bread
  • 1/2 ripe avocado
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • A pinch of red pepper flakes (optional)
  • 1 egg
  • 1 tbsp crumbled feta cheese
  • 2 cherry tomatoes, halved
Instructions:
  1. Toast the bread until lightly browned.
  2. Bring a small pot of water to a simmer and poach the egg (3–4 minutes until white is set).
  3. Mash the avocado; season with salt, pepper, and red pepper flakes if using.
  4. Spread avocado on toast, top with the poached egg, feta, and cherry tomatoes. Serve immediately.
Lunch: Monday Dinner Leftovers
Instructions:
  1. Reheat the leftover lemon herb chicken with quinoa and sautéed veggies (about 2 minutes in a microwave or on a skillet) until warmed through.
Dinner: Baked Cod with Tomato Olive Relish, Couscous & Sautéed Zucchini (Yields leftovers for Wednesday’s lunch)
Ingredients (for 2):
  • For Cod & Relish:
    • 2 cod fillets (6 oz each)
    • Salt and pepper, to taste
    • 2 tbsp olive oil (divided)
    • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
    • 1/2 cup Kalamata olives, pitted and chopped
    • 1 small red onion, finely chopped
    • 2 garlic cloves, minced
    • 1 tbsp capers (optional)
    • 1 tbsp chopped fresh basil (or parsley)
  • For Couscous:
    • 1 cup couscous
    • 1 cup water or broth
  • For Zucchini:
    • 2 medium zucchinis, sliced into rounds
Instructions:
  1. Preheat & Season: Preheat oven to 400°F; season cod with salt and pepper.
  2. Tomato Olive Relish: In a bowl, mix cherry tomatoes, olives, red onion, garlic, capers, and basil with 1 tbsp olive oil.
  3. Bake Cod: Place cod on a parchment-lined baking sheet, spoon relish on top, and bake for 12–15 minutes until flaky.
  4. Prepare Couscous: In a saucepan, bring water (or broth) to a boil, stir in couscous, cover for 5 minutes, then fluff.
  5. Sauté Zucchini: In a skillet, heat 1 tbsp olive oil over medium heat, add zucchini, sauté for 3–4 minutes, season lightly.
  6. Plate & Serve: Divide couscous between two plates, add cod with relish, and arrange zucchini on the side. Cool leftovers for Wednesday’s lunch.

Wednesday

Breakfast: Overnight Oats with Almond Milk, Figs, Honey & Pistachios
Ingredients (for 1):
  • 1/2 cup rolled oats
  • 1 cup almond milk
  • 1–2 dried figs, chopped (or 1 small fresh fig, chopped)
  • 1 tbsp honey
  • 2 tbsp chopped pistachios
Instructions:
  1. Combine oats and almond milk in a jar or bowl.
  2. Stir in figs and honey.
  3. Cover and refrigerate overnight (or at least 6 hours).
  4. In the morning, stir well and top with pistachios before serving.
Lunch: Tuesday Dinner Leftovers
Instructions:
  1. Reheat the baked cod with tomato olive relish, couscous, and sautéed zucchini until heated through.
Dinner: Chickpea & Spinach Stew with Quinoa & Greek Salad (Yields leftovers for Thursday’s lunch)
Ingredients for Stew (for 2):
  • 1 can (15 oz) chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 can (14.5 oz) diced tomatoes (or 2–3 fresh tomatoes, chopped)
  • 3 cups fresh spinach (packed)
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1/2 tsp smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
Ingredients for Quinoa:
  • 1 cup quinoa (rinsed)
  • 2 cups water or vegetable broth
Ingredients for Greek Salad (for 2):
  • 1 cucumber, diced
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1/2 small red onion, thinly sliced
  • 1/4 cup Kalamata olives, pitted
  • 2 oz feta cheese, crumbled
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp red wine vinegar
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
Instructions:
  1. Prepare Quinoa: In a saucepan, combine quinoa and 2 cups water (or broth). Bring to a boil, reduce heat, cover, and simmer for 15 minutes. Fluff with a fork.
  2. Make the Stew: In a large pan, heat 2 tbsp olive oil over medium heat. Sauté chopped onion for 3–4 minutes until softened. Add garlic and cook for 1 minute. Stir in diced tomatoes, chickpeas, cumin, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper; simmer for 8–10 minutes. Stir in spinach and cook until just wilted (about 2 minutes).
  3. Prepare Greek Salad: In a bowl, combine diced cucumber, chopped tomatoes, red onion slices, and Kalamata olives. Drizzle with olive oil and red wine vinegar; season with salt and pepper. Top with crumbled feta cheese.
  4. Plate & Serve: Divide quinoa between two plates, spoon the chickpea-spinach stew over the quinoa, and serve with Greek salad. Cool leftovers for Thursday’s lunch.

Thursday

Breakfast: Mediterranean Omelette with Spinach, Tomatoes, Olives & Feta
Ingredients (for 1):
  • 2 eggs
  • A small handful of fresh spinach, roughly chopped
  • 1 small tomato, diced
  • 1/4 cup sliced olives (Kalamata or your choice)
  • 2 tbsp crumbled feta cheese
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1 tsp olive oil
Instructions:
  1. Whisk eggs with salt and pepper.
  2. Heat olive oil in a nonstick skillet over medium heat; add spinach and tomato, sauté for 2 minutes.
  3. Pour in eggs and allow to set slightly, stirring gently.
  4. Add sliced olives and feta; continue cooking until eggs are set. Fold the omelette and serve immediately.
Lunch: Wednesday Dinner Leftovers
Instructions:
  1. Reheat the chickpea & spinach stew with quinoa and Greek salad until warmed through.
Dinner: Grilled Salmon with Roasted Vegetables & Bulgur Wheat (Yields leftovers for Friday’s lunch)
Ingredients (for 2):
  • For Salmon:
    • 2 salmon fillets (6 oz each)
    • Salt and pepper, to taste
    • Lemon slices (from 1 lemon)
  • For Roasted Vegetables:
    • 1 small eggplant, cubed
    • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
    • 1 zucchini, sliced
    • 2 tbsp olive oil
    • 1 tsp dried thyme (or oregano)
    • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • For Bulgur Wheat:
    • 1 cup bulgur wheat
    • 1 1/2 cups water or vegetable broth
    • A pinch of salt
Instructions:
  1. Preheat Oven: Preheat oven to 425°F.
  2. Roast Vegetables: On a baking sheet, toss eggplant, red bell pepper, and zucchini with olive oil, thyme, salt, and pepper. Roast for 20–25 minutes (stir halfway) until tender and lightly browned.
  3. Prepare Bulgur Wheat: In a saucepan, bring water (or broth) with a pinch of salt to a boil. Stir in bulgur wheat, cover, and let sit for 12–15 minutes. Fluff with a fork.
  4. Grill Salmon: Season salmon with salt and pepper. Heat a grill pan or skillet over medium-high heat; cook salmon for 4–5 minutes per side. In the final minute, top with a lemon slice.
  5. Plate & Serve: Divide bulgur wheat and roasted vegetables between two plates. Top with a grilled salmon fillet. Reserve leftovers for Friday’s lunch.

Friday

Breakfast: Fresh Fruit Salad with Greek Yogurt, Honey & Chia Seeds
Ingredients (for 1):
  • 1/2 cup plain Greek yogurt
  • 1 cup mixed fresh fruit (leftover berries, orange segments, apple chunks, etc.)
  • 1 tsp honey
  • 1 tsp chia seeds
Instructions:
  1. Combine the mixed fresh fruit in a bowl.
  2. Stir in the Greek yogurt until evenly mixed.
  3. Drizzle honey and sprinkle chia seeds on top. Serve immediately.
Lunch: Thursday Dinner Leftovers
Instructions:
  1. Reheat the grilled salmon with roasted vegetables and bulgur wheat until warmed through.
Dinner: Mediterranean Tuna Pasta with Capers, Olives, Cherry Tomatoes & Spinach; Served with Garlic Bread
Ingredients (for 2):
  • For Pasta:
    • 8 oz whole-grain pasta
    • 2 cans tuna in olive oil (5 oz each), drained (reserve a little olive oil)
    • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
    • 1/2 cup Kalamata olives, sliced
    • 2 garlic cloves, minced (plus extra for garlic bread)
    • 2 cups fresh spinach
    • 1 tbsp capers, rinsed
    • 2 tbsp olive oil (plus any reserved)
    • Salt, pepper, and red pepper flakes (optional)
  • For Garlic Bread:
    • 1 small baguette or slice of whole-grain bread
    • 1 garlic clove, minced
    • 1–2 tsp butter or olive oil
    • A few chopped fresh parsley leaves
    • Optional: a light sprinkle of salt
Instructions:
  1. Cook Pasta: Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil, cook pasta until al dente (reserve 1/4 cup pasta water), then drain.
  2. Prepare Sauce: In a skillet over medium heat, warm 2 tbsp olive oil and sauté minced garlic for 1 minute. Add cherry tomatoes, olives, and capers; sauté 2–3 minutes until tomatoes soften. Stir in tuna and fresh spinach; if needed add reserved pasta water to loosen. Season with salt, pepper, and red pepper flakes.
  3. Combine Pasta & Sauce: Toss pasta into the skillet until well coated.
  4. Prepare Garlic Bread: Preheat broiler. Slice baguette in half lengthwise; mix minced garlic with butter (or olive oil) and parsley. Spread on bread halves and broil 2–3 minutes until lightly toasted.
  5. Plate & Serve: Divide tuna pasta between two plates and serve with garlic bread.
