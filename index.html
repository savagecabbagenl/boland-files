Monday

Breakfast: Fig & Honey Greek Yogurt Bowl
Show/Hide Breakfast Recipe

Ingredients

  • 2 cups full-fat Greek yogurt
  • 2 fresh figs, quartered
  • 2 tbsp honey
  • ½ cup granola
  • ¼ cup walnuts, chopped

Recipe Directions

  1. Spoon Greek yogurt into two bowls.
  2. Top with quartered figs, drizzle honey, sprinkle granola and walnuts.
  3. Serve immediately.

Nutritional Information (Per Serving)

  • Protein: ~21 g
  • Carbs: ~40 g
  • Fats: ~11 g
Dinner: Herb-Roasted Chicken with Farro Salad & Roasted Mediterranean Veggies
(Leftovers for Tuesday Lunch)
Show/Hide Dinner Recipe

Ingredients

Herb-Roasted Chicken

  • 2 boneless, skinless chicken breasts (7 oz each)
  • 2 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • Salt & pepper, to taste

Farro Salad

  • 1 cup farro, rinsed
  • 2 cups water or low-sodium broth
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1 small cucumber, diced
  • ½ red onion, finely chopped
  • 1 cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • Juice of ½ lemon
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt & pepper, to taste

Roasted Vegetables

  • 1 medium zucchini, cubed
  • 1 red bell pepper, seeded & cubed
  • 1 small eggplant, cubed
  • 1 tbsp olive oil, salt & pepper

Recipe Directions

  1. Marinate Chicken: Toss chicken with olive oil, lemon juice, garlic, thyme, salt, and pepper. Let sit for 30 minutes.
  2. Roast Chicken: Bake or grill at 425°F (220°C) until internal temperature reaches 165°F (74°C), then slice.
  3. Cook Farro: Simmer farro in water or broth for about 25 minutes until tender; drain.
  4. Prepare Salad: Toss farro with cherry tomatoes, cucumber, red onion, parsley, lemon juice, olive oil, salt, and pepper.
  5. Roast Veggies: Toss zucchini, bell pepper, and eggplant in olive oil, salt, and pepper; roast in a 425°F (220°C) oven for 25–30 minutes.
  6. Assemble: Plate sliced chicken over farro salad with roasted vegetables on the side. Reserve extra for Tuesday’s lunch.

Nutritional Information (Per Serving)

  • Calories: ~520 kcal
  • Protein: ~42 g
  • Carbs: ~45 g
  • Fats: ~18 g

Tuesday

Breakfast: Spinach & Tomato Frittata
Show/Hide Breakfast Recipe

Ingredients

  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 cup fresh spinach, chopped
  • ½ cup diced tomatoes
  • ¼ cup crumbled feta
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt & pepper, to taste

Recipe Directions

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Beat eggs with salt and pepper; stir in spinach, tomatoes, and feta.
  3. Heat olive oil in an ovenproof skillet and pour in the egg mixture.
  4. Bake for 12–15 minutes until set.

Nutritional Information (Per Serving)

  • Calories: ~310 kcal
  • Protein: ~23 g
  • Carbs: ~8 g
  • Fats: ~20 g
Lunch: Leftover Monday Dinner
Dinner: Baked Cod with Lemon-Caper Sauce & Mediterranean Veggie Medley
(Leftovers for Wednesday Lunch)
Show/Hide Dinner Recipe

Ingredients

Baked Cod

  • 2 cod fillets (≈6 oz each)
  • 1 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • Salt & pepper, to taste
  • 2 lemon slices (plus extra juice if desired)

Lemon-Caper Sauce

  • ¼ cup Kalamata olives, chopped
  • 1 tbsp capers
  • ¼ small red onion, finely diced
  • Juice of ½ lemon
  • 1 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil

Mediterranean Veggie Medley

  • 1 small eggplant, cubed
  • 1 small zucchini, cubed
  • 1 red bell pepper, cubed
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • Olive oil, salt & pepper

Recipe Directions

  1. Bake the Cod: Brush fillets with olive oil, season with salt and pepper, top with lemon slices, and bake at 425°F (220°C) for 12–15 minutes until flaky.
  2. Prepare Sauce: Combine olives, capers, red onion, lemon juice, and olive oil in a bowl.
  3. Roast Veggies: Toss eggplant, zucchini, bell pepper, and cherry tomatoes with olive oil, salt, and pepper; roast in a 425°F (220°C) oven for 20–25 minutes.
  4. Assemble: Serve the baked cod drizzled with the lemon-caper sauce alongside the roasted vegetable medley. Reserve extra for Wednesday’s lunch.

Nutritional Information (Per Serving)

  • Calories: ~480 kcal
  • Protein: ~36 g
  • Carbs: ~32 g
  • Fats: ~22 g

Lunch: Leftover Tuesday Dinner
Dinner: Lemon Garlic Shrimp with Bulgur Pilaf & Steamed Green Beans
(Leftovers for Thursday Lunch)
Show/Hide Dinner Recipe

Ingredients

Lemon Garlic Shrimp

  • 12 large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • Salt & pepper, to taste
  • 1 tsp chopped fresh parsley

Bulgur Pilaf

  • 1 cup bulgur wheat
  • 2 cups water or broth
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tbsp chopped fresh parsley
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt, to taste

Steamed Green Beans

  • 8 oz green beans, trimmed
  • A pinch of salt
  • 1 tsp olive oil (optional)

Recipe Directions

  1. Sauté Shrimp: In a skillet, heat olive oil and sauté garlic for 1 minute. Add shrimp, lemon juice, salt, and pepper; cook until shrimp turn pink (about 3–4 minutes). Stir in parsley.
  2. Prepare Bulgur: In a saucepan, sauté chopped onion in olive oil until soft. Add bulgur and water or broth, bring to a boil, then simmer for 15 minutes until tender. Stir in parsley and season with salt.
  3. Steam Green Beans: Steam green beans until crisp-tender (about 5 minutes), then toss with a pinch of salt and olive oil if desired.
  4. Assemble: Plate the shrimp over a bed of bulgur pilaf with a side of green beans. Reserve extra for Thursday’s lunch.

Nutritional Information (Per Serving)

  • Calories: ~480 kcal
  • Protein: ~38 g
  • Carbs: ~40 g
  • Fats: ~16 g

Show/Hide Breakfast Recipe

Ingredients

  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 cup fresh spinach, chopped
  • ¼ cup crumbled feta
  • ½ cup diced tomatoes
  • 1 tsp olive oil

Lunch: Leftover Wednesday Dinner
Dinner: Vegetable & Chickpea Tagine with Couscous
(Leftovers for Friday Lunch)
Show/Hide Dinner Recipe

Ingredients

Vegetable & Chickpea Tagine

  • 1 small onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 medium carrot, sliced
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 small zucchini, sliced
  • 1 can diced tomatoes (14.5 oz)
  • 1 can chickpeas, drained
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • ½ tsp ground cinnamon
  • ¼ tsp turmeric
  • Salt & pepper, to taste
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped (for garnish)

Couscous

  • 1 cup whole-grain couscous
  • 1 cup boiling water
  • 2 tbsp chopped fresh parsley
  • Salt & a drizzle of olive oil

Recipe Directions

  1. Sauté Base: In a large pot, heat olive oil and sauté onion and garlic until soft. Add carrot, bell pepper, and zucchini; cook for 5 minutes.
  2. Add Main Ingredients: Stir in diced tomatoes, chickpeas, cumin, cinnamon, turmeric, salt, and pepper. Simmer for 15–20 minutes until flavors meld.
  3. Prepare Couscous: Place couscous in a bowl, pour boiling water over it, cover for 5 minutes, then fluff and stir in parsley and a drizzle of olive oil.
  4. Assemble: Spoon the tagine over couscous and garnish with chopped cilantro. Reserve extra tagine for Friday’s lunch.

Nutritional Information (Per Serving)

  • Calories: ~510 kcal
  • Protein: ~18 g
  • Carbs: ~65 g
  • Fats: ~16 g
