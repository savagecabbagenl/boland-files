Mediterranean Meal Plan

Monday to Friday

Monday
Breakfast: Greek Yogurt Parfait
Ingredients (for 1):
  • 1 cup plain Greek yogurt
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 1/4 cup walnuts, roughly chopped
  • 1/2 cup mixed berries
Instructions:
  1. Spoon Greek yogurt into a bowl or glass.
  2. Drizzle honey over the yogurt.
  3. Sprinkle walnuts on top.
  4. Gently fold in or layer the mixed berries. Enjoy immediately.
Dinner: Lemon Herb Chicken with Quinoa and Sautéed Veggies (Yields leftovers for Tuesday’s lunch)
Ingredients (for 2):
  • 2 boneless, skinless chicken breasts (6 oz each)
  • 1 lemon (zest and juice)
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 tbsp olive oil (divided)
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/4 tsp black pepper
  • 1 cup quinoa (rinsed)
  • 2 cups water or low‐sodium chicken broth
  • 1 small head broccoli, cut into florets
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • Optional: Fresh parsley (for garnish)
Instructions:
  1. Marinate Chicken: Combine 1 tbsp olive oil, lemon juice, lemon zest, garlic, oregano, salt, and pepper. Add chicken; marinate 20 minutes (up to 1 hour in fridge).
  2. Prepare Quinoa: In a saucepan, combine quinoa and water (or broth). Bring to a boil, reduce heat, cover, simmer for 15 minutes. Fluff with a fork.
  3. Cook Chicken: Preheat a grill pan or skillet over medium-high heat. Cook chicken for 5–6 minutes per side until internal temperature reaches 165°F.
  4. Sauté Veggies: In a skillet, heat 1 tbsp olive oil over medium heat. Add broccoli; sauté for 4–5 minutes. Add cherry tomatoes in the last 2 minutes. Season lightly with salt and pepper.
  5. Plate & Serve: Divide quinoa between plates, slice chicken, and top with veggies. Garnish with parsley. Cool leftovers for Tuesday’s lunch.
Tuesday
Breakfast: Mediterranean Avocado Toast with Poached Egg
Ingredients (for 1):
  • 1 slice whole-grain bread
  • 1/2 ripe avocado
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • A pinch of red pepper flakes (optional)
  • 1 egg
  • 1 tbsp crumbled feta cheese
  • 2 cherry tomatoes, halved
Instructions:
  1. Toast the bread until lightly browned.
  2. Bring a small pot of water to a simmer and poach the egg (3–4 minutes until white is set).
  3. Mash the avocado; season with salt, pepper, and red pepper flakes if using.
  4. Spread avocado on toast, top with egg, feta, and tomatoes. Serve immediately.
Lunch: Monday Dinner Leftovers
Instructions:
  1. Reheat Monday’s leftover lemon herb chicken with quinoa and sautéed veggies (about 2 minutes) until warmed through.
Dinner: Baked Cod with Tomato Olive Relish, Couscous & Sautéed Zucchini (Yields leftovers for Wednesday’s lunch)
Ingredients (for 2):
  • For Cod & Relish:
    • 2 cod fillets (6 oz each)
    • Salt and pepper, to taste
    • 2 tbsp olive oil (divided)
    • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
    • 1/2 cup Kalamata olives, chopped
    • 1 small red onion, finely chopped
    • 2 garlic cloves, minced
    • 1 tbsp capers (optional)
    • 1 tbsp chopped fresh basil (or parsley)
  • For Couscous:
    • 1 cup couscous
    • 1 cup water or broth
  • For Zucchini:
    • 2 medium zucchinis, sliced into rounds
Instructions:
  1. Preheat & Season: Preheat oven to 400°F; season cod with salt and pepper.
  2. Make Relish: In a bowl, combine tomatoes, olives, onion, garlic, capers, and basil with 1 tbsp olive oil.
  3. Bake Cod: Place cod on a parchment-lined sheet, spoon relish over fillets, and bake 12–15 minutes until flaky.
  4. Prepare Couscous: In a saucepan, bring water to a boil, stir in couscous, cover for 5 minutes, then fluff.
  5. Sauté Zucchini: In a skillet, heat 1 tbsp olive oil, add zucchini, sauté for 3–4 minutes until tender, season lightly.
  6. Plate & Serve: Divide couscous between plates, add cod with relish and zucchini. Cool leftovers for Wednesday’s lunch.
Wednesday
Breakfast: Overnight Oats with Almond Milk, Figs, Honey & Pistachios
Ingredients (for 1):
  • 1/2 cup rolled oats
  • 1 cup almond milk
  • 1–2 dried figs, chopped (or 1 small fresh fig, chopped)
  • 1 tbsp honey
  • 2 tbsp chopped pistachios
Instructions:
  1. Combine oats and almond milk in a jar or bowl.
  2. Stir in figs and honey.
  3. Cover and refrigerate overnight (or at least 6 hours).
  4. In the morning, stir well and top with pistachios before serving.
Lunch: Tuesday Dinner Leftovers
Instructions:
  1. Reheat Tuesday’s baked cod with relish, couscous, and sautéed zucchini until heated through.
Dinner: Chickpea & Spinach Stew with Quinoa & Greek Salad (Yields leftovers for Thursday’s lunch)
Ingredients for Stew (for 2):
  • 1 can (15 oz) chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 can (14.5 oz) diced tomatoes (or 2–3 fresh tomatoes, chopped)
  • 3 cups fresh spinach (packed)
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1/2 tsp smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
Ingredients for Quinoa:
  • 1 cup quinoa (rinsed)
  • 2 cups water or vegetable broth
Ingredients for Greek Salad (for 2):
  • 1 cucumber, diced
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1/2 small red onion, thinly sliced
  • 1/4 cup Kalamata olives, pitted
  • 2 oz feta cheese, crumbled
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp red wine vinegar
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
Instructions:
  1. Prepare Quinoa: In a saucepan, combine quinoa and 2 cups water (or broth). Bring to a boil, reduce heat, cover, simmer 15 minutes. Fluff with a fork.
  2. Make the Stew: In a large pan, heat 2 tbsp olive oil over medium heat. Sauté chopped onion for 3–4 minutes until softened. Add garlic (1 minute), then stir in tomatoes, chickpeas, cumin, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper; simmer 8–10 minutes. Stir in spinach; cook until just wilted (about 2 minutes).
  3. Prepare Greek Salad: In a bowl, combine cucumber, tomatoes, red onion, and Kalamata olives. Drizzle with olive oil and red wine vinegar; season with salt and pepper. Top with feta cheese.
  4. Plate & Serve: Divide quinoa between two plates, spoon the stew over quinoa, and serve with Greek salad. Cool leftovers for Thursday’s lunch.
Thursday
Breakfast: Mediterranean Omelette with Spinach, Tomatoes, Olives & Feta
Ingredients (for 1):
  • 2 eggs
  • Small handful fresh spinach, roughly chopped
  • 1 small tomato, diced
  • 1/4 cup sliced olives (Kalamata or choice)
  • 2 tbsp crumbled feta cheese
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1 tsp olive oil
Instructions:
  1. Whisk eggs with salt and pepper.
  2. Heat olive oil in a nonstick skillet over medium heat; add spinach and tomato, sauté 2 minutes.
  3. Pour in eggs, let set slightly while stirring gently.
  4. Add olives and feta; continue cooking until eggs are set. Fold and serve immediately.
Lunch: Wednesday Dinner Leftovers
Instructions:
  1. Reheat the chickpea & spinach stew with quinoa and Greek salad until warmed through.
Dinner: Grilled Salmon with Roasted Vegetables & Bulgur Wheat (Yields leftovers for Friday’s lunch)
Ingredients (for 2):
  • For Salmon:
    • 2 salmon fillets (6 oz each)
    • Salt and pepper, to taste
    • Lemon slices (from 1 lemon)
  • For Roasted Vegetables:
    • 1 small eggplant, cubed
    • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
    • 1 zucchini, sliced
    • 2 tbsp olive oil
    • 1 tsp dried thyme (or oregano)
    • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • For Bulgur Wheat:
    • 1 cup bulgur wheat
    • 1 1/2 cups water or vegetable broth
    • A pinch of salt
Instructions:
  1. Preheat Oven: Preheat oven to 425°F.
  2. Roast Veggies: On a baking sheet, toss eggplant, bell pepper, and zucchini with olive oil, thyme, salt, and pepper. Roast for 20–25 minutes (stir halfway) until tender and lightly browned.
  3. Prepare Bulgur: In a saucepan, bring water (or broth) with a pinch of salt to a boil, stir in bulgur, cover, and let sit for 12–15 minutes. Fluff with a fork.
  4. Grill Salmon: Season salmon with salt and pepper, then grill in a pan over medium-high heat for 4–5 minutes per side. Top with a lemon slice in the final minute.
  5. Plate & Serve: Divide bulgur and roasted veggies between plates, top with grilled salmon, and reserve leftovers for Friday’s lunch.
Friday
Breakfast: Fresh Fruit Salad with Greek Yogurt, Honey & Chia Seeds
Ingredients (for 1):
  • 1/2 cup plain Greek yogurt
  • 1 cup mixed fresh fruit (leftover berries, orange segments, apple chunks, etc.)
  • 1 tsp honey
  • 1 tsp chia seeds
Instructions:
  1. Combine the mixed fruit in a bowl.
  2. Stir in Greek yogurt until well mixed.
  3. Drizzle with honey and sprinkle chia seeds on top. Serve immediately.
Lunch: Thursday Dinner Leftovers
Instructions:
  1. Reheat the grilled salmon with roasted veggies and bulgur wheat until warmed through.
Dinner: Mediterranean Tuna Pasta with Capers, Olives, Cherry Tomatoes & Spinach; Served with Garlic Bread
Ingredients (for 2):
  • For Pasta:
    • 8 oz whole-grain pasta
    • 2 cans tuna in olive oil (5 oz each), drained (reserve some olive oil)
    • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
    • 1/2 cup Kalamata olives, sliced
    • 2 garlic cloves, minced (plus extra for garlic bread)
    • 2 cups fresh spinach
    • 1 tbsp capers, rinsed
    • 2 tbsp olive oil (plus any reserved)
    • Salt, pepper, and red pepper flakes (optional)
  • For Garlic Bread:
    • 1 small baguette or slice whole-grain bread
    • 1 garlic clove, minced
    • 1–2 tsp butter or olive oil
    • A few chopped fresh parsley leaves
    • Optional: light sprinkle of salt
Instructions:
  1. Cook Pasta: Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil, cook pasta until al dente (reserve 1/4 cup water), then drain.
  2. Prepare Sauce: In a skillet, heat 2 tbsp olive oil and sauté minced garlic for 1 minute. Add tomatoes, olives, and capers; sauté 2–3 minutes until tomatoes soften. Stir in tuna and spinach; if needed, add reserved pasta water. Season with salt, pepper, and red pepper flakes.
  3. Combine: Toss pasta in the skillet until well coated with the sauce.
  4. Garlic Bread: Preheat the broiler. Slice the baguette lengthwise, then mix garlic with butter (or olive oil) and parsley. Spread evenly on the bread and broil for 2–3 minutes until lightly toasted.
  5. Plate: Divide the pasta between plates and serve with garlic bread.
