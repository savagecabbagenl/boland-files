Meal 1: Grilled Lemon-Herb Chicken with Quinoa Tabbouleh

Ingredients

For the Chicken

  • 2 boneless, skinless chicken breasts (≈6–8 oz each)
  • 2 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

For the Quinoa Tabbouleh Salad

  • 1 cup quinoa, rinsed
  • 2 cups water or low-sodium chicken/vegetable broth
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1 medium cucumber, diced
  • ½ red onion, finely chopped
  • 1 cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • ¼ cup fresh mint leaves, chopped
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • 2 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • (Optional: ½ cup crumbled feta cheese)

Recipe Directions

  1. Marinate the Chicken: Whisk together olive oil, lemon juice, garlic, oregano, salt, and pepper. Coat the chicken breasts and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.
  2. Cook the Quinoa: Bring water or broth to a boil, add quinoa, reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for 15 minutes until liquid is absorbed. Let cool.
  3. Prepare the Tabbouleh: In a large bowl, mix quinoa, tomatoes, cucumber, red onion, parsley, and mint. Drizzle with lemon juice and olive oil, season with salt and pepper. Optionally stir in feta.
  4. Grill the Chicken: Preheat your grill to medium-high and cook the chicken for 6–7 minutes per side until it reaches 165°F (74°C). Let rest and slice.
  5. Serve: Plate the tabbouleh and top with sliced grilled chicken.

Nutritional Information (Per Serving)

  • Calories: ~500 kcal
  • Protein: ~40 g
  • Carbohydrates: ~40 g
  • Fats: ~18 g

Meal 2: Baked Salmon with Olive-Caper Salsa & Roasted Mediterranean Vegetables

Ingredients

For the Baked Salmon

  • 2 salmon fillets (≈6 oz each)
  • 1 tablespoon extra-virgin olive oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 2 lemon slices (plus extra juice if desired)

For the Olive-Caper Salsa

  • ¼ cup pitted Kalamata olives, roughly chopped
  • 2 tablespoons capers, drained and chopped
  • ¼ small red onion, finely diced
  • 2 tablespoons fresh parsley, chopped
  • Juice of ½ lemon
  • 1 tablespoon extra-virgin olive oil

For the Roasted Mediterranean Vegetables

  • 1 small eggplant, cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 1 zucchini, sliced into half-moons
  • 1 red bell pepper, chopped
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes
  • 1 small red onion, cut into wedges
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil
  • Salt, pepper, and dried oregano (or basil) to taste

Recipe Directions

  1. Preheat Oven & Prepare Vegetables: Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C). Toss eggplant, zucchini, red bell pepper, cherry tomatoes, red onion, and garlic with olive oil, salt, pepper, and oregano on a baking sheet. Roast for 25–30 minutes, stirring halfway.
  2. Prepare the Salmon: Place salmon in a greased baking dish. Drizzle with olive oil, season with salt and pepper, and lay a lemon slice on each fillet. Bake for 12–15 minutes until salmon flakes easily.
  3. Make the Olive-Caper Salsa: Combine olives, capers, red onion, parsley, lemon juice, and olive oil in a bowl. Adjust seasoning as needed.
  4. Assemble the Dish: Spoon the salsa over the baked salmon and serve with the roasted vegetables.

Nutritional Information (Per Serving)

  • Calories: ~550 kcal
  • Protein: ~35 g
  • Carbohydrates: ~30 g
  • Fats: ~30 g

Grocery List

  • Proteins: 2 boneless, skinless chicken breasts; 2 salmon fillets
  • Produce: 3 lemons; 4 garlic cloves; 1 small eggplant; 1 zucchini; 2 red bell peppers; 1–2 small red onions; 2 cups cherry tomatoes; 1 medium cucumber; 1 cup fresh parsley; ¼ cup fresh mint leaves; (optional: extra basil or oregano)
  • Grains: 1 cup quinoa
  • Pantry & Condiments: Extra-virgin olive oil; Salt; Pepper; Dried oregano; Capers; ¼ cup pitted Kalamata olives
  • Dairy (Optional): ½ cup crumbled feta cheese
  • Broth (Optional): Low-sodium chicken or vegetable broth
